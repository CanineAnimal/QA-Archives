Because this involves a real person, I'll explain the legal situation in this country instead of giving a direct answer.

In Themiclesia, a person claiming self-defence to excuse themselves from homicide charges must satisfy that (1) the threat to life is reasonably believed to be real and imminent, (2) the threat is unavoidable, and (3) that the action taken in avoidance of said threat is the option of least damage. That is, if you have a both a lethal and non-lethal option, both being available and adequate, to deal with a threat to your life that is reasonably believed to be real, imminent, and unavoidable, you cannot select the lethal option because it is not the option of least damage; if you select it in the presence of an available non-lethal option, you're guilty of murder. If you have a pistol that you can fire at someone meaning to kill you, or you have a bomb that will do the same and also blow up a few more houses, you must select the former; if you select the latter, you're guilty of murder. The excuse of self-defence must also be proportionable to the threat present: if someone enters your property or robs your safe without an intention to kill you, you cannot justify the killing of that person as a defence of your right to your movables or to enjoy your real property.
