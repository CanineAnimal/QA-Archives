It’s a somewhat complicated question. First off, a gun like it wouldn’t be considered a self-defense or sporting firearm (Classes 3 and 4), since the phrasing implies personal ownership, public and commercial purposes (Class 1) are probably out of the question. Class 2 firearms can only be obtained as military/police surplus or registered domestic products, of which the PP-19 Bizon is neither, so it cannot be legally obtained for personal use in El Lazaro. Since illegally imported weapons are also unlicensed, possessing one in another crime in itself. Finally, if you were arrested because you were carrying an (unlicensed) Class 2 firearm in public, unapproved usage and storage of Class 2 firearms is another crime. Worse, if you smuggled the weapon yourself across borders, you’re guilty of arms trafficking.
