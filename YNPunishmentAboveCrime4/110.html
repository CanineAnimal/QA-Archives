Ausslaugand wrote:Grand Larceny; Auto Theft


No specific analogue in Salvarojense law, due to the general prohibition against individual ownership of automobiles and general legal framework of Salvaroja’s socialist property laws.

One analogue would be “Private Ownership of a Capital Good.” This offense would encompass a person trying to maintain possession of an automobile that does not already belong to a collective authority of some kind. This is a civil infraction mainly punishable by expropriation of the illegally possessed automobile. In some cases this maybe accompanied by up to a 500 cupón laboral fine.

A second analogue would be “Abuse of Collective Property.” This would apply for a person who has a right to use the car, but fails to return it when required. For instance, a member of a collective farm who signs one of the farm’s cars for a week, but refuses to bring it back within that time frame, is subject to a penalty of “rehabilitative labor necessary to correct the abuse,” usually amounting to community service equivalent to the time in which the car was used beyond the authorized time.

A final analogue would be “Aggravated Abuse of Collective Property.” This would apply to someone who either takes an automobile from its collective owner without any right, or with an apparent purpose to permanently and unlawfully appropriate it. Such an individual is liable to be put to “Confined and Directed Rehabilitative Labor” for a period of up to three years, though usually only enough time to generate “labor value equivalent to the good so misappropriated, times two.”
