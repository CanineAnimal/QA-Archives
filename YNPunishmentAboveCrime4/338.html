Petty Theft (value under one day's mean average wage) -- replace the item, plus pay owner cost of item, plus a usage fee of reasonable cost to lease the item, plus 1 day hard labor for every day stolen item is not returned to owner up to 1 year hard labor. Item to be garnished from wages if thief cannot repay immediately.

Theft (value over one day's mean average wage but under one month's mean average wage in value) -- replace the item, plus pay owner cost of item, plus an usage fee of reasonable cost to lease the item, plus 1-5 years hard labor, Item to be garnished from wages if thief cannot repay immediately.

Grand Theft (value over three years' mean average wages) -- 5 years hard labor, replace the item, plus pay owner cost of item, plus a usage free of reasonable cost to lease the item, plus waged garnished if thief cannot pay immediately.

These are the typical maximum sentences. Judge has discretion for lighter sentencing. In some cases up to 20 years hard labor may be given for recidivism. In all cases thief's assets can be liquidated to pay for restitution upon discretion of the judge.
