Depends on the type of public property. We can generally classify public property as 'used' and 'unused'. So, like, large forests that aren't nature reserves are owned by the government and are considered 'unused', so you can do what you like there. As for 'used' property, if its for resource harvesting or a nature reserve, you can ask for permission but will probably be denied. You won't face punishment unless it actually caused harm, but it would be destroyed and you would be removed.

As for public buildings like hospitals and government buildings, or even publicly owned factories or such, which are increasingly uncommon these days, the worst case is that it gets in the way of its purpose, and you get forcibly removed. If it isn't in the way of anything, you can go right ahead.
