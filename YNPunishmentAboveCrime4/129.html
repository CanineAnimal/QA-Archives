Law is a complex beast at the best of times. It gets even more complicated for how non-government organizations like us handle such things. Now, first of all, we operate in a lot of different countries, each of which have different laws. So expect this answer to reflect that fact. Most of the charges have different names than what you gave, but that's a different story. Anyways...

Regarding the following descriptor you list:

(including the president)

President isn't a political office in our setting, it's a university office only. Democracy doesn't exist in our setting, in any form. The very concept doesn't exist. While using debate to decide something is an understood concept, voting is not. (The debate is usually just so that the person who is in charge of the decision can make an informed decision.)

We'll pretend you said "king", absolute monarchy is extremely popular in our setting as a political system, and it even comes in a wide variety of forms, including the slightly more unusual "estate monarchy" where succession on the throne is decided by the monarch's last will and testament, and it doesn't have to go to a blood relative either.

Alternatively, we can pretend you meant one of our bosses, who are called "caput". This likewise is not a democratically elected position.

Regarding the following charges you list:

Illegal Possession of Melee Weapons
Illegal Possession of Firearms
Illegal Possession of Explosive Weapons
Illegal Possession of Incendiary Weapons

Within the many countries we operate in, the only way to illegally possess these things is if you stole them, which is covered under the charge of theft. There is no illegal way to own personal weaponry because personally weaponry isn't even restricted, let alone outlawed. In general, the governments of our realm recognize the need for self defense and the existence of a militia, and thus place no restrictions on owning weaponry. (Though considering what monsters are known to exist, this makes sense.) In some places, it is illegal to not own a personal weapon.

Regarding the following charges you list:

Illegal Usage of Melee Weapons
Illegal Usage of Firearms
Illegal Usage of Military Vehicles
Illegal Usage of Explosive Weapons
Illegal Usage of Incendiary Weapons
Illegal Usage of Bio-Weapons

We just call that "Assault with a Deadly Weapon". There aren't specific charges for any of those. If you meant something other than using them to attack people, it might be covered under some other charge. Either way, these charges as listed don't exist.

Regarding the following charges you list:

Illegal Possession of Military Vehicles
Illegal Possession of Bio-Weapons

Some countries we operate in don't have this as a thing. Others do, but lump both together as "wrongful possession of military equipment". Whether it's allowed or not generally depends on how much that country depends upon private militia for national defense.

Regarding the following charges you list:

Illegal Possession of Drugs
Illegal Usage of Drugs

Drug laws are far, far more lax over here. There has been no "war on drugs". Opium, cocaine, and marijuana can be purchased from your local druggist, and do not require a prescription. There are a few drugs which are restricted or outright illegal in a number of countries, but it's worth considering that they are well above and beyond what you would OOCly be used to. The prevailing societal reaction to drugs is that most of them are safe in moderation, and they don't really care if you hurt yourself with them as long as they don't hurt other people; this is reflected in drug laws. (One of the few drugs that is illegal in many places, fairy dust, is specifically outlawed because of how much of a danger fairy dust users pose to other people.)

Regarding the following charges you list:

Illegal Alcohol Consuming

That's not a thing in any of the countries we operate in. Generally speaking, over here, your options for beverages are alcohol, coffee, or tea. Water is oftentimes not safe to drink. As a result of this, there is no minimum age requirement set on alcohol consumption, though the alcohol that children drink is usually a far lighter fare which can barely be considered alcoholic. (Alcohol, coffee, and tea are safe to drink because the water has been boiled. We know these beverages are safe, but we don't know that boiling the water is what makes them safe.)
