Built as the 712nd Gearing-class destroyer of the United States Navy.
Having missed combat during World War II, Gyatt is arguably best known for being the world's first-ever guided missile destroyer; her refit from 1955 to 1956 most notably added a twin Terrier guided missile launcher to her aft end, which later caused her hull number to be changed to DDG-1 the next year. She served in this form for 6 years, but soon regressed into a conventional anti-submarine role from 1962 to 1963 similar to her sister ships.
She would be placed into the Select Reserve by 1968, stricken from the Naval Register a year later, and finally sunk as a target off Virginia in 1970
