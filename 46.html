A truly fascinating case, and a paragon of dedication and tenacity.
OVERALL ASSESSMENT: POSITIVE

I do wonder how exactly her father died. To die so suddenly of natural causes, while in good shape without notable ailments, seems strange. OVERALL ASSESSMENT: NEGATIVE
Regarding Sandro Mèndez:
Is there such a thing as "too much glory"? Apparently, at least for Mèndez, there is. I would never expect such a man, having earned such fame and prestige as an athlete, to not only become a lawyer of all things but to call it his passion - then again, if it wasn't his passion, there'd hardly be any reason for him to take it up. Of course, his actions and stances since entering his new career are rather basic and expected - fighting corruption, something everyone except its beneficiaries sees as "good", and decentralising the legal system and supporting individual rights, an understandable, unsurprising gesture from a populist "outsider" of the political system. Obviously I rather disagree with his beliefs, but he seems well-intentioned and kind. Not exactly someone I'd have in political office, but still a good, upstanding character.
