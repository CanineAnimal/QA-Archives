Temptations of Power

In a quaint white apartment with an open window by New Hope's boy sat a British man reading "The Prince" by Niccolo Machiavelli. He intently read pages of "Il Principe" to learn every detail of the book and the lessons taught befitting of a prince. Before putting the book away and contemplating on the political state of affairs in the South Cape. It's always the same in the People's Council - infighting, bickering, and factionalism among the different leftists. They had a less-than-friendly Nazi neighbor to the east and whenever, Lawrence tried to get the Council to wage war against the Brigade; his every proposal was shut down due to fear of casualties.

Lawrence understood very well the fear of mounting casualties in an open war against them. Yet the Council's infighting opened his eyes to the strength that could be displayed in power if ever so slightly. Machiavelli once said it is "better to be feared than loved" but Lawrence is uncertain if he could be that Prince. To make a powerplay against them and betray the ideals that he has fought for? It is simply heresy to him. Yet as he sat down and placed his head onto the table; a familiar voice comforted. Le Patron's voice and in his own dreams, he vividly saw the figure of Konrad in white standing next to him like a ghost of Old Patagonia.

As if the ghost of Konrad taunted him and fed his temptations. Soliloquying him with the misfortunes of the Council's tunnel vision and his beliefs that man would fall to depths of depravity if pushed a little towards the edge... Yet Lawrence had none of it. He stood up, shut his book tight, and held his head high in a show of domination against the ghost of his old friend. Standing there and becoming Premier is already enough evidence that Konrad philosophy of man having an untapped potential of evil as false. Calmly stated to Patagonia's ghost "No, you were the only one who is basking in your own delusion". For Konrad, it didn't matter if thousands fell to their depths but it was Lawrence which he attempted to prove his philosophy.

Yet Lawrence persisted in his ideals and his resolve strengthened. Unfazed by his illusionary demon, Konrad faded from Lawrence's vision like a gust of wind lost to time.
