Regarding Angie Pasorelli:
Ah, a police detective. Finally someone I can sort of relate to. Maybe. Can't say I've ever really shared much of her idealism in catching criminals, though people like her are definitely valuable. There's no motivator quite like genuine zeal, and she seems to have kept hers all throughout her life. She's fully committed to her post and does whatever it takes to get the job done - definitely a good fit for a high-ranking, specialised unit like hers. Given all the partners she's lost, it seems like she's mired in a crime haven of a country, but she only gets more motivated by this instead of backing out from the constant danger. Pretty admirable for her dedication, at the very least.
OVERALL ASSESSMENT: NEUTRAL
Regarding Israel Michaelis:
Abandoning secret police and mass surveillance in favour of police militarisation across the board? ...Why? So citizens can have a bigger target to direct their resentment to? It doesn't seem like it's doing much to make them like him, and they get the psychological impression of an "overbearing police state" either way - much more so, in fact, with this approach, and to far less benefit to the state. The officer down the street wearing riot gear and carrying a rifle is a hell of a lot more glaringly obvious than a mass surveillance network, and doesn't give you any of the knowledge. It's just stupid. All this just to differentiate himself from his predecessor in the eyes of the public, how sad. It's like he's not good enough at anything else, so this is his botched way of trying to get mass appeal.
OVERALL ASSESSMENT: NEGATIVE
