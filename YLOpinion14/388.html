Jean-Andou Flaubert
Head of State of France
Report/Opinion:
... What do we have here? Wait, what? What the hell are these words....

Oh, no matter. Hello there, Anšar. My name is Jean-Andou, though you can call me Andou. I see that you've been through hell, just like me. Suffering is universal, it seems, my story may be too dissimilar to yours, but I appreciate your service to your homeland. It seems as though your life was one marked by delinquency during the early days...Oh. Now I see why. It's personally not hard for me to get over the loss of my parents, but if I may, I should say my condolences. Your parents must have been good people, I'm sure of it. Unlike mine...

Petty crime. We've all been through that phase once in our lives, don't worry, though I can't remember if I actually participated in hooligan-like activities. If I may, I'll say this, petty crime is a path that leads to fifteen minutes of fame at best, and at worst? Just a simple answer, death. I'll say that while it might not be out of pure 'good', it is still admirable of you to pay back the people you owe, as most people I see outsourcing their work don't even bother to pay their employees. I may not have the best view on things, but it's still an admirable quality, rewarding the people who help you.

Joining the Military is not an easy feat, let me tell you. I lost my father to it, and I'd rather he was permanently lost, but with enough patience and courage, you can become a great war hero. It seems that you did just exactly that, becoming a hero for your actions. A younger part of me would have ignored the rest of the military service you put, but right here? I can definitely see more than just big explosions and wonderful guns. A part of me feels... infuriated knowing that the man you've been trying to help was untrustworthy, but you tried your best. I can respect that, it takes a lot to try convincing people to turn back from crime, as some... some just don't accept and die for it.

And entering the realm of politics is an even harder feat than the military. Let me say that. It's full of treachery, wickedness, malevolence, and corruption... but on that note, it's also filled with people trying their best, opportunists wanting to improve the nation even if it means sacrificing their lives, and good-hearted people wanting a place in the nation's history. With a person like you in charge, your nation has no chance of falling. I can say that for sure. A leader with strong support and passionate speeches is worth more than the barrel of a gun. I wish you the best of luck with your rule.

On politics... I understand your conservativeness, a centuries-long doctrine that keeps everyone happy is better than a revolutionary ideology with large ambitions and only words backing it. I may be one whose ideology would be considered foreign by your standards, but then again, I could say the same about your ideology, however with all respect, I shall not. Civil militarism? An interesting subject, and a hard one to tackle at least over here. Some people, cowards I presume, refuse to pick up a gun, and instead whine about how all guns are dangerous and should be banned. I'm not one of them, thankfully.

All and all, not a bad leader, though a bit too conservative, it's still a good fit for the nation.

Farewell, from France!
