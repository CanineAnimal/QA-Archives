Ministry on Foreign and Internal Affairs
Supercomputer Tom
Opinion on the Alastair
Status: Not Classified, for public's eye, if necessary





Huh, what do we have there? A poor soul led down a horrible rabbit hole by alcohol. He might be a monarch, but it is indeed sad to see how low a human can land, and not only affect themselves, but their family as well. Anyways, let's unpack what he has on the table.

Preborn, with pneumonia and lung damage at such young age? If I was a human, I perhaps could even feel a lot more pity about such a hard childhood. Like a lot of other royals, his life seems to have been sheltered - a balance between lavish and spartanist lifestyle is the best. That said, I don't particularly care about his school/university adventures.

His career before him becoming a king seems to have been related to law things, and he seems to have even been good enough at it. His frustration of not being able to run for the post he wanted is understandable, but such is a life for someone who is in a direct succession to a throne. Luckily, we freed some such individuals back at the home from such misery, and their souls hopefully reached their loved ones. His father's death seems to have come at a wrong time, though - it intensified something he was struggling with already for years.

At last to the politics... He seems to be somewhat ''conservative'', particularly on political change and abortion, but so far he still seems like a milkquetoast democratic leader with which world is full. His economicss are the same ''planned economy bad, but laissez-faire also bad'', and there is nothing surprising. I would also say that he is too democratic, but he would maybe take that as a compliment... If his nation lasts, his people are up for a rude awakening.

Regards,
I
Supercomputer Tom, the General Secretary, General of the Mankind, and Eternal President of WSRSC
