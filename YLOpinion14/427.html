"The High Priestess, and Ketorushima in general, provide an interesting anthropological case-study with regards to the economic and political development of early societies. From the perspective of historical materialism, I would place Ketorushima in an early stage of development, given the social organisation and the beginning of external economic relations. Perhaps a good comparison could be made with the Haudenosaunee in pre-Columbian North America.

Anyway, with regards to Mai Chie herslef, I am afraid that it is somewhat difficult for me to engage with her beliefs and policies on equal terms, given the vast gap in socio-historical development between our respective societies. I suppose that I would consider her to be reasonably progressive given her circumstances, and there is little I would particularly object to in her beliefs besides the focus on spiritual idealism over materialism, but again, that isn't unsurprising given the societal context within which she exists.
