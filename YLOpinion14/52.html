As I've stated before, my view on John Mercer is somewhat favourable - from an ideological standpoint, he seems to be more in line with my own thinking than most foreign leaders I've encountered, and his stance on transparency is a welcome change from the overly-heavy-handed obfuscation approach many autocrats tend to employ. His recognition of the fragmentation and collective weakening caused by libertarianism is commendable, such foresight is certainly a good quality for a leader to have. That said, a lot of his claimed positions appear seem self-congratulatory with little meaning in my eyes. Being in favour of "meritocracy", against "corruption", "incompetence", and "mindless warmongering"; such terms have very little significance without further elaboration. How is "meritocracy" applied? How is "mindless warmongering" defined? These views could be ascribed to anyone. Come to think of it, though, I can't honestly claim to be against truly mindless warmongering. I wouldn't take Khoronzon to war on a whim without further consideration, of course, but war itself is something I find beauty in, and I wouldn't lose respect for someone who recklessly charges into battle purely for the sake of causing destruction, foolish and futile as it may be - unless that someone is serving my nation, naturally.
