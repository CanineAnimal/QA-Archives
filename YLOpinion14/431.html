Statement on the Feyrisshire 'High Princess' Reissechi Yuri

Polish People's Republic
Jeszcze Polska nie zginęła!



How weird it is that a leader of a socialist nation not only is a young woman, but is also a monarch, of some sort. Although, having read into the function of the Feyrisshire state and government, the position of High Princess seems to be little more than an elected premiership with a crown, and is really just the place as the head of the government within the country. So perhaps I am wrong about the High Princess being a monarch as I am not familiar with the Feyriss nation's culture and language after all. After having looked into the policies of Reissechi Yuri I do believe that the interests of the High Princess are aligned with those of the socialist world, even if I find myself disagreeing with her state's celebration of such figures as former Soviet General Secretary Josef Stalin and even the foolish Mao Zedong. Stalin made many mistakes during his rule, and to cling to him would be an endorsement of mismanagement and cult of personality. I do not hate the late General Secretary, but I find his method of ruling to be one of senseless paranoia and unnecessary purges. But of course Stalin did make the USSR into a great world power, and he was instrumental in the victory of the people over fascism and in the liberation of Poland and the fraternal nations. Mao on the other hand was a rogue figure whose ideas mangled the core tenets of Marxism-Leninism and brought devastation to the Chinese economy, even when he was given the aid of the entire socialist world to build China in the first place. I find no reason to support such figures who split the international socialist movement in favour of their own idiocies and misrule.

As for the woman herself, I think that she is too young to be leading the nation, but if the Feyriss Communist Party disagrees with me then so be it. Her own rule has seemingly resulted in a repeat of Stalinist problems, such as mass purges and large scale repression of more agreeable individuals within the party. I do, however, agree with the abolition of economic liberalizations, as the state of the proletariat must centralize the Means of Production as to avoid the law of value being the key regulator of production. If the Feyriss seek to develop socialism within their country then Poland will actively support them in their endeavours, although hopefully the High Princess (I still find that using such a term to describe the premier of a socialist state to be bemusing) will eventually learn from the mistakes of Stalin and come to her senses surrounding the question of Mao Zedong and this 'anti-revisionism' (Albanian?) nonsense in favour of collaborating with other socialist nations in their work for the betterment of mankind.

The Polish United Workers' Party sends its greetings to the Feyriss and to High Princess Chief Secretary Reissechi Yuri, hopefully we may be able to meet in person to discuss your policy and path towards socialism. Best of regards to your nation in its attempts to construct a people's nation, and a warm welcome to the socialist world from the working people of Poland. Remember that the world socialist movement is stronger when all nations march in unity and not when it is split up by fools and wavering leaders.


Signed by the First Secretary of the Polish United Workers' Party, Roman Kor
