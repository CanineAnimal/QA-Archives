Russian Security Council

Ataman Stepanov's thoughts on Damien Blackwell

Damien Blackwell seems like an... interesting person. Him being a minister of science and technology already means that he has a very bright mind, yet his physical form is perhaps the most confusing - his head and hands (?) look like that of a grey wolf's, sure. But the rest of his body seems rather humanoid in appearance, and he has got some not-so casual attire as well. Either way, his life story needs to be known first.

The first time he appeared was about 50,000 years ago, so it implies a pretty long history and experience. It wouldn't be surprising if he was even older. It seems that he led a mechanization effort or something like that in the tribal societies? Either way, he kickstarted a golden age (or rather steam age) in his world, even if people in it didn't knew how exactly it was pulled off. Speaking of Octavius the Conqueror, some similarities can be seen within him and Free Territory's foreign policy, as both end up in conquests of large land swaths. Concepts such as Temple of Olympus and Sanctuary of Gods sound like straight up taken from mythology books, but in Damien's world people treat those things seriously as they do exist - unlike the abrahamic ''gods'' and other elements of darkness, which are just fiction at best, and a people's opiate at worst.

As for how Damien rose to his position... The battle description before was kind of boring - yeah, battles do rage on like thunders. Anyways, him and the king being absent certainly started to have a toll on Alkdorian war effort, but at least he came in handy the moment when Alkdorians needed him the most. That dreadnought which blasted the enemies from skies and the rest of mechanical warfare was surely possible thanks to Damien's engineering thinking. Hiding his effort in winning the war seems like an attempt to reduce his importancy at first, but there is surely a better reason why it remains an open secret. Overall, while the emperor Octavius is not that all impressive, the Technology and Science minister is the opposite.

On internal political sphere he is pretty apathetic - sounds like he could potentially be one of those Black Army's generals which have their loyalty or greed way above their competence level, except that Damien actually has competence as well and does so only to let others figure out things themselves and put his focus on the Empire's wellbeing. It reminds of a certain general named Batov, who lost in power struggle with Yeltsin. Him being a minister of Technology and Science, it makes sense for him to pour all that money for new military designs and weapons, although the idea of ''anti-magic'' warfare sounds like something to snicker about, at least in our world. Either way, WMDs are often the best way of how to turn a massively losing fight into your favor. Last, but not the least - his pragmatic approach to combat is desirable, as ''honorable'' warfare is an ideological fool's game.

Not much info was found out about Blackwell's personal life, so any judgements and thoughts about his personal life should be put on hold until we can manage to achieve better intel.

In conclusion, Damien is an alright figure in the position he currently is in, and as Damien is no danger to Russian Free Territory, opinion on him shall remain neutral with a cordial lean until further notice.

Ataman Stepanov's thoughts on Kzan "Zen" Aigystos Kaisar

Once again the encounter is with a person with both humanoid and animal features, but Agiystos form is actually kind of... cute? Especially when considering that quite a few people view him as a piece of sh*t for being what seems like a human supremacist and a supremacist of some other race, called Ignisians. The Free Territory doesn't concern itself with the dynamics of space entities and alien species as of now, as neither of them come at play at the big picture on Earth, but that already doesn't paint Kaisar in a particularly good light. However, more of what is in Kaisar's store should be evaluated.

Kaisar seems to have been born in Siberia, or Urals to be more specific, but other than that there is little known about his past life - sounds a bit suspicious. Either way, what matters are the things he did when he got his hands on power - Free Territory does not believe in something such as ''possession'' of a body, but apparently this is what happened when Kaisar took over a body of a robotics researcher. When he brought Ignasae together in a single powerful empire, Kaisar also twisted Marxist thought according to his needs - a brilliant move, just like Stepanov hijacked the experiment from inside out. Last but not the least, Earth appears to have been the first target for his expansionist empire - while the thought of an another copy of Earth somewhere out there being conquered by aliens sounds disheartening, at the same time he seems to have fought against some ''demons'' which had tormented the earth with their chaos and hatred, the conquest is more justifiable. Kaisar's decision to integrate the remaining humans as fully equal citizens of the empire is also commendable, and one of the few things that all of humans across the multiverse will thank him for.

To sum things up, a bunch of more conquests followed after the first serious war took place on Earth, and at one point Zen had no major enemies left - all of the larger empires were annexed, with some heavy local population culling in the process. And yet, Zen still thought that it wasn't enough, he desired to fully conquer the multiverse. That said, all things have to come to an end - and in this case it came from a catastrophic loss after he wanted to save what was left of his home multiverse. At least Zen got a second chance after his blunder, but not the soldiers which vanished as well. While Zen's mistake was being sentimental of his home, it is almost guaranteed that some other disastrous event would have eventually led to the downfall of Ignisian Empire.

On political matters, his past policies were quite... mixed. Zen had the right idea when it came to maintaining an expansionist empire to further ones power and dealing with the remains of monarchy - parasitic nobles and royals have no place for a well-engineered machine. However, him insisting on environmental protection is a bit baffling - the environment is the oyster of sentient species, up for them to exploit in order to advance the luxury and living level in their lands, and the greens have been an annoyance so far. His current desire of ''Rule of Time and Void'' speaks of grand delusions and/or narcissism, however - he thinks that he owes all the power and respect, and will be ready to stomp on others in the way to achieve his goals even if it comes at a cost of fellow human and Ignisian lifes. Hey, that's where the old Stepanov can find something in common.

Zen's robotic tone and honest voice would make him a great paper-pusher or a writer of formal documents, really. Thus it comes to no surprise that he could be perceived as someone who is always cold hearted, even if it is not true most of the time. From one side, not dealing with the crap from unintelligent and lazy people sounds like the best approach, but from other side it is not as fun when you are on the receiving end of bashing. Not caring less about the personal lives of strangers and bashing down those who cross his way without mercy sounds a lot like how the Black Army operates, but it is still obvious that he has all the flaws that come with being a human on inside.

In conclusion, Aigystos Kaisar's wielded powers and political influence make him a dangerous entity, with lots of potential having a risk of being used towards not-so good goals, especially the ability of destroying a whole universe. His speciesist approach to the space also means that some particularly unsavory individuals could have a fertile ground under his empire. This alone makes him a subject of which Free Territory should wary, so the opinion on Zen is negative until further notice.
