Honestly, it reminds me of the Occidental Revolutions in Europe, Ireland specifically. Though, I do think there should be a place for unions post-revolution, with syndicates representing both cooperatives and unions. I also think there should be a separation of powers between geographic and vocational constituencies.

Another criticism I have of the government is the Chairman being an ad hoc executive. I think the position is ripe for an abuse of power at worst and easy to become something like the Prime Minister at worst. That and the need for delegates in an age that has the Internet is questionable. I guess they could be around in case of emergencies such as a pandemic where traditional citizen assemblies can't assemble, but having the votes of many local assemblies tallied is honestly a better system. Delegates, when given power, can easily become plain-old representatives.

The lack of checks and balances in general make the Irish system of Connors vulnerable to the concentration of power, though the lack of a political party dominating the process does help in that respect. Oh and the Democratic Party in the RCAS is not like the Bolshevik party. It is a lot more loosely organized with a larger member base. I wouldn't even call it a traditional political party, since it doesn't control legislation due to the lack of a parliament. If there was a representative body in the RCAS that the Democratic Party could've dominated, then I would worry about the party becoming too powerful. Though, I guess it would've eventually split if it were like that. In essence, the Democrats post-Risorgimento is less of a cohesive party with whips and more of a vestigial organization. Politics in the RCAS tends to be rather non-partisan outside of the executive branch, since issues are voted on directly by citizens. Sectional and economic interests predominate within the political system.

Allowing for wage labor without any restrictions is a dangerous prospect. 'Cultural distrust' would do little to protect workers, especially if the world situation is not in favor of Ireland. Apprenticeships and exemptions for small/family businesses can be done to fill in the holes that wage labor had traditionally fulfilled before capitalism's rise.

I do admire his opposition to theocracy, and his liberal interpretation of Catholicism effectively makes Christianity less of an issue. Though, I do think local governments should have more discretion in what laws they can make when it comes to 'crimes'. That and drugs have historically caused huge problems in America. Now, criminalizing the use isn't the answer. The answer instead should be rehabilitating drug users and cracking down on the dealers. That and regulating the pharmaceutical industry. This may go against Connors' laissez-faire approach, but I think any market needs to be regulated to some extent.

As for the man himself, his life story is honestly weird. It's based entirely on his politics and implementing said politics into the country. It's boring frankly. And- HOLY CRAP, he got into power at 34 years old?! What the actual fuck?! I spent almost a hundred years to start a truly socialist revolution in America and this fucker does it magically in a decade or two. I call bullshit! This is an outrage! And, how did the globalist capitalists NOT invade his country to restore the ancien regime? I have so many questions!"
