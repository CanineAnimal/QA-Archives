Jeane Valentine 'The New Franco' Sabre
President of the United States [2006-2010]
Report/Opinion:
I shuffle the paperwork on my desk, and I return to see a fresh face. At least, as fresh as 10-day old Salami can be. Well, you saved me the trouble of responding to that... freak, so I'll take my time for this one.

From what opening text I see, I read what appears to be a man with a complicated past. What I get from looking it in a more recent light, however, is the fact that this document is a sign of the early-2000s and mid-2010s. The... 'edge' phase, as my son James says. I can see where he's coming from, looking back at books and articles written from now and those years. It really is a testament of time to see something that would be seen as 'cool' and 'hip' with a dash of 'awesome' is now just seen as an attempt to make a supreme character who tramples over everything like it's a massive steamroller or a flying brick that does... absolutely nothing. God, now I remember why my kids refuse to read Young Adult novels despite their classmates shilling it out to them constantly. I feel like an old literary critic nowadays, but hey, as long as people go look for their epic, strong, authority-leaking character with absolutely all enemies and all the methods to beat them... Good lord, I'm giving terrible writing advice.

Right then. An exile, a criminal, a scientist, a servant, and a king. What do they have in common? They're all Kal'Shazzar, a man who reads more like an Undertale OC if it was made during the hey-day of that part of the fandom. According to my kid, the part of the fandom where the fanbase would ship knife-wielding maniacs and make them absolutely bullshit overpowered. At the very least, I see an excellent researcher here, but not an excellent protagonist for a novel. I mean, come on, five minds controlling one body is too overpowered to actually be challenging unless the author managed to find a way to make it more nuanced and have the minds split into different arguments over different things. But... well, what I instead have to read is a man who had to go to exile. A bunch of people who had to go searching for the secrets of the universe and being surprised when finding out the secrets of this God-given universe bites them in the ass and gets them exiled.

At the very least, Kal'Shazzar got to see the stars and the galaxy at war with itself. But... still, somehow being designated a war criminal as a distinction really doesn't bode well. In fact, I'd say that the way this document is written overestimates the actual worth of Kal'Shazzar in favor of a dramatized, raunchy and violent story about a man going out and doing some bullshit tasks. I... holy shit, this really does read like a dramatized story. The textual tone... yeah, it doesn't paint mister Kal'Shazzar in the best of lights for me if he has to emphasize 'WORSHIPPED' and other associated words. This... really does read more like a propaganda piece. The... jeez, ow the edge. There it goes leaking into my writing. You know what? I'll say this. Mister... you may seem like a man with troubles and a rocky road ahead of you, but as of now, I simply cannot find the time to believe such a story. It is simply too absurd to be honestly true. I've... put down the document. Maybe someday, I can analyze this in more detail. But not now.

From the desk of the revolutionary ex-president, Jeane Valentine Sabre.
