Russian Security Council

Ataman Stepanov's thoughts on Foresittend Marcus Alan Ariel Maximillian “Unendlichkeit Jäger” Swifty Felix Maverick Prometheus Revlin Lynx Artyom Lichtenstein Baptiste C. Talon Haydn Lincoln Taal Reynolds Fang Wu Ying

The individual in question really has got a mouthful word, haven't they? One of the first things which can be noticed is their absurd height - their pre-Valentinian height already puts up them way above average, but the Valentinian one is just a three centimeters away from the tallest man in the world - their name was Robert Wadlow or something like that. One would obviously also be concerned if Reynolds has any health problems related to such height, as there is a higher risk of cancer and more blood clots. Their fight armor is nothing like our world has seen yet in real life, and which would only appear in a sci-fi book. Either way, we must look way past those superficial things.

His scientific background and participation in politics reminds of Andrey Sakharov - a nuclear physicist who led the Modernist salon in Tomsk. Now, unlike Sakharov, Reynolds seems to be way less elitist and focus on people's personal prosperity, despite still channeling funds for scientific grade projects (which is absolutely fine). Reynolds also has the correct opinion when it comes to welfare and personal freedoms, as it is generally a waste of time and resources to police what citizens do with their personal lives if it doesn't cause political damage. That said, there is a disagreement between the two sides when it comes to religion - Stepanov thinks that religions are the opiates of the people, thus their practice should be clamped down as much as possible. And it seems that he got his armor in the first place from a specimen which was from an entirely different universe - things just became a lot more confusing there, along with the usage of worms for armor, even if there is likely a good reason behind that.

Stepanov decided to take a look at their stats and try to evaluate - at least this time it didn't require one of the number guys to explain in a language for average Free Territory's citizen. Reynolds HP, damage and firing rate per second look pretty absurdly impressive at glance, but judging by the fact that pretty much everyone in Valentinian world has way higher stats than humans on Earth, it would be a stretch to call Reynolds as the strongest person which Valentinians can muster. Reynolds usage of melee attacks and shield would be incredibly archaic if those functioned like regular swords and shields, but apparently his style of fighting still does a good job for him when considering the damage rates. For most other stats there is nothing much else to do than nod in understanding.

Overall, Reynolds seems like a pretty apolitical, yet powerful being which should not be taken lightly. Even if he is not a fighter in his personal life, with those abilities of his he could probably be used as a killing machine by a more... sinister government, and he is lucky that Valentinians have already unified the Earth and are relatively peaceful, instead of sending the super soldiers to claim more lands and spill blood in process. To sum it up, Stepanov's thoughts on Reynolds are indifferent-cordial, because both sides can interact with each other only through the multiverse warps.
