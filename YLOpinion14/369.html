Ataman Stepanov's thoughts on Fukushima Sonoko

The person in question is a Premier who also is an agnostic - some of the militant anti-theists would interpret it as plain cowardice, but Free Territory really has no businesses with the personal beliefs of foreign leaders, unless it comes down to invade them and greet with a lump of lead. Either way, the fact that the regulation of magnetic tape production is the biggest thing over which Baizou's parliament and council fight shows that things are indeed alright for the most part, as more time is spent on such trivial, everyday things. Of course Fukushima knows when to keep her mouth shut and bridge the gap, so the choice for grand coalition was really obvious. Fukushima could probably be a great mediator between conflicting families and couples if she wasn't a premier. Or perhaps just return to her positions at management - that business graduate degree seems to have worked well for her.

So, she is also the youngest premier in her country's history? Well, 38 years is still quite a lot, and there seem to be quite a few leaders which are even younger than that - in fact, the good old (ha!) Companion Galanskov was only 25 back at 1964 when the Black Army started to expand, and that teen spirit during the Siberian Black Army uprising burned like a fierce fire. Last, but not the least - Fukushima's policies. Welfare funding and civil liberties are pretty sound policies, especially if they are like bread and circuses to distract the population. Paying for transitional surgeries still sounds like something that's too early to implement in the Free Territory, even if those under the letter of T in acronym are accepted with acceptance being enforced. Overall, she is one of those liberal/social democratic leaders who will probably go down into history as decently average and benevolent, with if not pristine, then at least not too stained record.
