On Ben Kerry

My, this is a novelty. I have seen reluctant monarchs, yes. With that sort of system, such a consequence is all but inevitable. But a reluctant military dictator? A curious case, certainly. But then, he isn't quite a dictator. The more I read into this, the more it appears to be just another case of involuntary military promotion - and since he does not qualify for a national leader, I cannot judge him as such. So then, let me judge him as a soldier. He appears competent, certainly. But I grow weary of his reluctance to obey orders. Yet at the same time... I cannot exactly fault him for it. History has shown us the consequences of incompetent officers. A soldier who stands against such things is left in a better position when the situation around him inevitably deteriorates... but then, stands at risk with regards to his own hubris, should his superiors turn out to be in the right. A word of warning for tovarisch Kerry - perhaps you should give orders more thought before rebelling against them. Informational compartmentalization often has merit - while I am not saying that, in every case, those who withhold information from their subordinates are justified in so doing, not all knowledge is of value, or even of help, to everyone in the chain of command. There are things out there that are better left unknown.

On Celine Amelia Pinky

A woman defined in her life story by a single, harrowing experience. A set of rather unfortunate events that, admittedly, led to her current inclusion in the Valentian diplomatic roster - an enviable position... but was the suffering worth it? It is difficult to imagine what exactly she went through, nor can I really relate in a direct manner. She has my condolences regardless, and I wish her a rapid recovery from any possible trauma she may still carry with her. There is, admittedly, not much to comment on from a political perspective, but at this point that seems to be irrelevant - as a rule of thumb, any Valentian is a worthy ally, and a valuable friend.
