Essentially, it forces the workers to exploit themselves, even if such exploitation is in some sense lessened by this system. To put it plainly, the hell of capitalism is the firm, not the fact that firm has a boss.

Secondly, I find your attachment to Sherechian spiritualism to be concerningly idealist and unscientific. I understand that you say you've met with some historical figures within the 'afterlife', and will give you the benefit of the doubt in assuming that this story isn't made up. However, the only real alternative I can see is that you were hallucinating in some way, possibly brought on by your circumstances at the time. Now, I'm not a psychologist, so I can't say whether this is a liability with regards to your ability to govern, but it's still a bit concerning. Still, you seem reasonably confident so I might be worrying over nothing. Stress gets to us all sometimes.

To conclude, while I respect you for overthrowing the old regime and enacting changes that are certainly progressive given Sherecia's material conditions, I cannot consider you to be a socialist comrade. The fact that you adhere to what is ultimately a capitalist economic system, despite its egalitarian ambitions, and promote unscientific idealist beliefs, holds you and Sherechia back from achieving true socialism. Still, there doesn't seem to be anyone in a good position to drive this transition right now, so you have my critical support for the moment."

    ~ Director-General Sophie Hawkins
