No socialist state has ever outright banned religion. It has even been supported and encouraged at times, like in the Soviet Union, where the church was sponsored to build up patriotism during the Second World War. Religion has often stood hand-in-hand with socialism in guiding revolutions around the world, such as in Libya with Gaddafi's Islamic Socialism and the PLO's war of Muslim liberation. Marx's description of it as an "opiate" was not meant to mock religion itself, but as a compassionate statement about how people are forced to turn to ideas like the divine in order to have any hope in the face of their miserable existence in poverty.

The colony was founded largely by folks descended from Mexico and the southwestern US. A lot of Aztec imagery is popularly woven into national feelings, and the figure of Yohaulticetl in particular served a symbol of hope during the centuries of company rule, when population was closely controlled and infants were often taken from their mothers by company doctors or schoolteachers. These doctors were largely company employees of German descent who used the practice of forced abortion and eventually kidnapping to control the distribution of races in the colony. Yohaulticetl, the "lady of the night" and protector of infants, was syncretized with the Catholic Virgin Mary as well as the Chinese Nüwa, and helped to galvanize the personal resolve of grieving people.
