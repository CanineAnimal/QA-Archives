Basically, the main ethos of Christian Anarchism(or Orthodox Israelism in this case) is basically the whole "Jesus is socialist" shit, but redact the "socialist" part with "Jesus was an anarchist". In this world, Tolstoy's influence on the ideological popularity of the ideology amongst the Orthodox community is much, much larger than what it was OTL. Some members of the Orthodox church, primarily the ROC, adopt these ideas, and seeing as it threatens the legitimacy of the Tsar, Okhrana, Russia's secret service at the time, receives orders from the Ministry of Interior to root out adherents of the ideology. Okhrana does that, which sparks a mini-revolution(termed the April 4th Uprising) in Vladivostok, and after the revolt, it sparks an emigré movement, similar to the White Movement after the Russian Civil War, that mainly fled to Orthodox-majority areas. Vojvodina, being a Orthodox-majority area, received the 4th most emigrés, ahead of Romania, but behind Serbia.

Why only Orthodox areas? The majority of emigrés thought that those ideas could only be applicable to Orthodoxy, but it later, albeit during what, instead of the Cold War in our timeline, was called "The Crisis", it spread to other Christian-majority areas by the late 50's. Cut to the Karađorđević dynasty getting word of Christian Anarchism's existence, some of them become enticed by it, and they decide to, during WW1, to revolt in Vojvodina, which later spread to all of Banat, which was what they viewed as "the Land of Christ", having claimed that instead of Christ being born in Judea, he was born in Banat, but made a trek across the Roman Empire due to him supposedly having word from Gabriel that God needed him there.
