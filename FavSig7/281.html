So many words I don't know the differences between!


Market socialism is technically socialism with workplace democracy and co-operatives, however unlike other socialist systems it includes a market, henceforth the name.
Democratic socialism is basically a big tent ideology of socialism combined with democracy. Most democratic socialists are reformists, meaning they support reform to achieve socialism, however some may be revolutionaries but still support a democratic end goal.
Social democracy used to mean the same thing as Democratic socialism, however most of the people today who espouse social democracy support a mixed economy (regulated capitalism combined with some state-owned industries, unlike social liberalism which only includes regulated capitalism) and a large welfare state. Most still see socialism as the end goal, though not as closely as Democratic socialists.
Left-wing populism is just populism combined with socialism. Populism normally also includes undermining of democracy and stuff like that, however I use "populism" to describe myself as just believing in some of the most common left-wing ideologies, to promote left unity.
Eco-socialism is just mainly democratic socialism, however with a more focused view on preserving the environment.
Libertarian socialism is much more radical than all the ideologies listed above, supporting anarchy and mostly communism, however I tend to find myself in the more radical part of the umbrella term, just support a smaller state instead of anarchy and mostly socialism instead of communism, however I tend to also find communism the ultimate end goal.
Reformist Marxism is basically democratic socialism but less utopic, and more focused primarily on Marxian economics. It differs from Orthodox Marxism and its derivates by supporting a reform-minded approach towards Marxism opposed to a revolutionary path. It is positioned to the left of democratic socialism, however, probably because of its more radical roots.
Alter-globalization is a form of the anti-globalization movement, opposing economic globalization but supporting globalization aimed towards the interests of humanity. It also supports preserving native cultures, international solidarity, and anti-austerity.
And no, I do not intend to write essays whenever I respond to a question.
