Richard Wetz - Symphony No 2

It appears I’ve grown fond of relatively obscure Romantic composers such as Zdeněk Fibich, Sir George Macfarren and now Wetz. Wetz’s music is very ‘Brucknerian’ as noted in the first line of his Wikipedia entry but I do notice some similarities to Franz Schmidt’s works. Wetz is quite an interesting character to me; in some ways I suppose he could be seen as an element of the 19th-century in the Weimar Republic and the interbellum Europe. So far, I’m impressed! I’ll listen to his other symphonies (especially his first one which I’m told is quite ‘dark’). Bohnke or Draeseke will follow but I still have to make a decision between the two.
