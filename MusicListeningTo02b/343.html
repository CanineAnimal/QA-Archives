People are seriously complaining about this album? LOL. What fools. Obviously these people don't understand the creative shift forward in Muse's musical direction. A direction which just so happens to parallel the way popular electronic music is going, but honestly guys, it's just a coincidence.
So far the only track I've heard from this album is "The 2nd Law: Unsustainable". And oh boy, what a track it is! The song starts with this string arrangement. To the untrained ear it may sound as blandly produced as stock music used in commercials, but this is obviously ironic. Muse knows it sounds bland, and the music is actually making a statement about how commercials are really bad and we shouldn't do what they tell us (Muse likes to infuse prolific messages into their work. More to come on this subject). We then hear the sound of a woman talking about the environment. I remember the first time I heard this song it completely warmed my heart because the environment is like really important and stuff and we really need to do something about it. So I was glad Muse was also in support of helping prevent the green house gassing and dangerous pollutions destroying our Earth.
So yeah, this woman is talking about something to do with the environment and then a robot appears and is like UNSUSTAINABLE and then the bass drops, but it isn't actually dubstep. There's this really heavy bassline that sounds exactly like dubstep except it's not actually dubstep because Muse is playing on guitars and not laptops. This is like how jazz isn't jazz when it's played on a guitar instead of a saxophone. I mean come on to everyone saying it's dubstep; how stupid do you guys have to be to think it is? So yeah there's this avant-garde non dubstep section with guitar noises that sounds like dubstep but it's not, but the important thing about this part is that the robot is saying "unsustainable" which is like a really important message about the environment and the green house gassings and pollutings. Muse obviously really care about saving the Earth from the people who are trying to damage it because they included this meaningful message in their song.
Now a lot of people have been criticizing the band for preaching environmental messages whilst being rich celebrities that don't give any of their massive income to supporting sustainable energy or anything like that, but this is actually wrong. My friend went past Matt Bellamy's house once and apparently he had a solar panel on his roof because he cares about the environment so much, so suck on that Muse-haters!
Overall this is definitely going to be the best album of this year and maybe even the best Muse record to date. I am really excited to hear this album in full.
Go Muse!

The main crap I'm sick of seeing is it saying they are 'selling out'. Dislike the songs, whatever. I don't care. But saying they are selling out makes me face palm. Yeah yeah yeah, they are using electronic sounds and are playing Dubstep on instruments, whatever. It's not like these Post-Rock bands that incorporate Electronic music into their songs are suddenly 'selling out' because they added electronic sounds. If anything, it can make the songs more epic. What I define as 'selling out' is like, say, P!nk's new album that, from what I've heard, literally sounds almost exactly like old P!nk. I don't consider an album that clearly has a varying amount of variety going for it (and we've only heard 3 songs of 13), with "Survival" being way different then "Madness" and "Unsustainable", as selling out. I haven't heard anything so far that makes me think they are selling out. I still get the impression they are evolving. Hell, I heard from them that there's parts on the new album that might be more on the metal level, so yeah.

You have people going like "Oh my god they still sound like Queen!" with Survival (which admittedly has shitty lyrics, but what can you expect from an Olympic theme song? It works). Then with Unsustainable, which being muse, I get the feeling they released first to troll their fans and concern them, which worked rather successfully, they go "Oh my god it's Dubstep!" and think they are going all Dubstep (even though the band said there's only like, one song with that on it), and want them to go back to old Muse, after complaining about the other song sounding like Queen and wanting them to change....Then, you got Madness, that's sort of like a chill pop song, which I'm pretty sure part of it is probably overly cheesy on purpose, with a random old-styled guitar solo thrown in, and people complain about that.

All I'm saying is I don't see how they sold out, and I still see them doing a vast variety of music (2 songs are sung by the bassist on this album, about his alcohol issues), and I think it's hard to judge the whole album based on 3 songs that sound drastically different then each other, which alone makes it not sound very much like 'selling out' in my opinion.
