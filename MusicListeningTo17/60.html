by Sword Island » Tue Apr 13, 2021 3:38 pm

I have been listening nonstop to Paramore for the last week. I had been familiar with a couple of their songs before I decided to dive into their discography, which was an amazing decision in my book. My first logical step was to look at their most recent album, After Laughter, and man was I hooked by the end of my first listen. My favorite songs from that album were Rose-Colored Boy, a song that blends an optimistic beat with an equally pessimistic message, and Hard Times, a song that gets stuck in my head way too easily and describes the 2021 state of mind perfectly even though it was released in 2018. After that, I went on a YouTube binge and wound up listening to a few songs from their self-titled album from 2013. The whole album in my opinion is both an homage to the band's pop-punk roots and a bridge to the new poppier but still emo Paramore. The song that I believe encapsulates this transition from being an angsty teenage band to an adult-oriented group is Ain't it Fun, a song all about the real world and the reality that you no longer have the safety blanket of home. And yet this same album presents a genuine pop love song with Still Into to You. The musical diversity of Paramore is really shown off in After Laughter and Self-Titled, not discounting All We Know Is Falling, Riot!, or Brand New Eyes. In my opinion, perhaps because I haven't listened to enough songs from this era of Paramore, AWKIS and Riot! seem to blend together, with the three songs I listened to from Brand New Eyes( The Only Exception, Playing God, and Brick by Boring Brick) having a different but similar sound to the first two albums. Speaking of the first two albums, man are they good. Misery Business, For A Pessimist I'm Pretty Optimistic, Emergency, crushcrushcrush, Born For This, and Fences have all made their way into my Spotify playlist.

And as a was relistening to the This is Paramore playlist for the nth time, I realized the common thread of my addiction to Paramore was Haley Williams' vocals. The one constant is her, and that isn't even constant. On some songs like Still Into You, her voice takes a poppier sound and in others like Misery Business, it's far more punk and much more in line with eyeliner and low cut jeans of the mid-2000s. But the most impressive thing about Paramore is that they were able to change their sound but not their identity. They're still the same band that released All We Know Is Falling just matured.





Sorry if this is long I just needed to get my thoughts out
