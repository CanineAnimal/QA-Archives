I was going to include this into my post, but I thought it was unnecessary. This prompts me though.

Ideology =/= Form of Government. Conservative is NOT descriptive of any form of government. It's an 'ideology' or even just a descriptor for those who want preserve the status quo. If that descriptor can change without actually changing the constitution de jure or de facto, then don't use it.

One-Party State is a form of government. Marxist-Leninist is not. Dictatorship is a form of government. Fascist is not. You can fascist one-party states and Marxist-Leninist dictatorships, and you can have Marxist-Leninist one-party states and fascist dictatorships. The Marxist-Leninist and fascist dictatorship may have different policies, but the structure of the government is the same. The inverse is true for the ideology thread. Segregate the ideology from the state, even if the state is ideological in nature. Fundamentally, a communist one-party state and a fascist one-party state share the same form of government, just radically different policies.

Everyone else here is doing it fine, much better than how it was before.
