Iceland is a weird place. The warmest it has ever got in the capitol on record was 79 degrees. But apparently going below 5 degrees in winter is very rare. Hell, looking at this, the record low for the capitol is -3. So it stays within a pretty contained temperature throughout like the entire year. 55 degrees in summer, low of 45 (record low in the hottest month of summer is 36). Winter average is a high of 35 and a low of 25. Temperature change isn't very drastic at all.

Where I live, 20 degree changes aren't exactly unheard of for weather in a day, if not more. And here, Iceland barely does 10

They don't even get much precipitation, either, apparently. 33 inch's a year (This is for the capitol, other locations are different). Though they do get snow every year I believe. And that's more precipitation than Seattle gets (Yes, Seattle gets less than that, despite being called the rainy state and rainy city. Not a lot of heavy precipitation, just lots and lots of cloudy days and light rain). I think where I live gets more, though...

Iceland is one of my top travel destinations, that's why I know so much about it I want to go there all thanks to music...
