Cult of Luna-Vicarious Redemption (Justin Broadrick Remix)

The story behind this songs existence is kinda funny:

"The remix of "Vicarious Redemption" by Justin Broadrick was not originally a part of the EP.[11] Cult of Luna's record label pressured them to include a remix on the release. The band caved into the label's demands and asked them to get Broadrick to remix "Vicarious Redemption" out of spite because, at almost 19-minutes long, it was the most difficult song to work with. Persson, who said he believes remixes to be a "waste of time," said Broadrick was chosen because he is "one of the few people I'd trust with a remix," and later praised his work on this song as an "amazing way of doing a remix."

Leave it to Justin Broadrick to remix a song in a good way (and make it 10 minutes shorter). His covers and remixes are so drastically different than the original. Which is a good thing. Covers and remixes that sound exactly like the originals are also the most boring usually.

And I agree. Remixes are a waste of time. Rebellious bands are the best
