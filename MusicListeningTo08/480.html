by Corrian » Thu Jul 10, 2014 3:16 am

God is an Astronaut-From Dust to the Beyond

At least back in the day, these guys rely less on generic crescendo post-rock and focus more on shorter post-rock songs that feel more like normal rock songs without the generic slow plodding into a generic crescendo like a lot of bands do. Which is what makes them a highlight to me.

Not to say they don't have that. But even they seem to go for more of a direct approach, instead of slowly developing into something massive. Their 2nd album, which was the first one I listened to by them, was one of my first post-rock albums beyond Sigur Ros (Explosions in the Sky being there somewhere), so it has a special place with me, anyway. But even that one is a more tolerable style of post-rock, even if it may be closer to "Generic post-rock". Still not as freaking generic as some of this crap.
