HMS Diadem
Lemartes Class Destroyer
International Waters west of Stevidian South Greal


HMS Diadem had been one of the first of her class when the Lemartes Class was first put into mass production to replace nearly every AA destroyer class in the Royal Navy. She was, and remained to this day, one of the most powerful destroyers in the entire world, her capabilities lethal and unquestioned; the flag she flew – feared. HMS Diadem was a symbol of Stevidian naval and technological engineering. Yet she sat idle in water after her encounter with RIN destroyer.

The Diadem had been on a routine patrol in international waters but the local regional tensions had changed her mission requirements. Captain O’Neill, a veteran from the last regional war with a career spanning 18-years, had ordered the Diadem to closely shadow a convoy belonging to Imbrinium. A cold war era tactic supposed to display power projection upon a foreign navy. This had elicited a hostile response from the escorting destroyer and after the Diadem ignored messages to leave (due to committing no international offence nor firing live rounds in provocation) was rammed by the enemy destroyer.

The result was significant damage to the stern of the Diadem. A minor hull rupture meant she was taking on water and the shaft damage was significant enough to kill almost all of her speed. Fortunately the Diadem was making back to port on ‘Get Me Home Speed’ on one prop shaft at low revs while damage control teams attended to the damage. The opposing destroyer suffered damage herself but visually looked unscathed – nevertheless she two had slowed to almost a complete stop.

O’Neill had called action stations and condition red and the ship, despite the damage, was fully combat operational. The HMS Diadem was now astern of the enemy vessel and it was no secret to their opponent that the only thing stopping the Stevidian destroyer from blowing them out of the water was the captain’s cool head. The King Arthur helicopter had her ASW torpedoes removed and replaced with a short-medium range sea skimming anti-ship missile; this same helicopter was now lurking with intent to destroy the opposing ship with but a signal light from Diadem. Additional threats came in the form of two Royal Marine scrambler speed boats each with 20 marines on board ready to assault the destroyer as soon as the word was given. They kept close the mother ship for the time being, but at top speed could close on the enemy and be ready board in less than 3 minutes.

The Imbrinium vessel would also be aware of the forward missile tubes housing Gothic Type I anti-ship missiles, proven to cripple even super dreadnoughts and developed out of lessons from the War of Golden Succession. More obvious yet was the deck gun that was pointing dangerously towards the opposing destroyer. On the deck machine guns were manned and lower ratings were armed with smaller machine guns and semi-automatic rifles.

HMS Diadem limped home leaving her counterpart to sit helpless but ready to fight. 3-hours away was a Royal Navy recovery ship on its way to transport the Diadem back to Stevid for a full repair and refit in lieu of the damage caused.

* * *


Back in Stevid, several emergency meetings had taken place with all heads of the armed forces and department heads from the Imperial Ministry of Defence. There had already been protests in the capital about the lack of action or even voice to towards the hostile states that were busy provoking Stevid into a war or words – and now a naval ramming.

But the discussions moved away from the ramming to more strategic affairs. Two satellite images and eight contact reports from submarines detailed huge ship movements believed to be from Imbrinium. In one of the satellite images a super dreadnought could be clearly seen and identified. However the actual force composition could not be ascertained (guessed to be 100+) and a satellite could not be moved into a geostationary position over north Greater Dienstad for another four days.

It was decided to stoke the fire further and no order was given to deploy a battle fleet despite one being present off east Morrdh that had been involved with Tehver crisis earlier in the year. This battle fleet was within easy striking range of Imbrinium and able to interdict any naval activities with just two days notice. This ace in the hole would be left unutilised for the time being, however its future use would probably come to pass. The fleet was ordered to move out further towards international waters and towards Imbrinium and conduct interstate war naval exercises and drills until needed.

In response to enemy fleet movements to Stevid’s north and east, three task forces would be dispatched from the 4th Fleet and would number around 30 fighting vessels, include a maximum of three carriers each, minus logistical support. These task forces would intercept and shadow enemy fleets at the maximum ferry range of the aircraft carried on the carriers.

"Would someone be kind enough to explain to me what the hell is going on?"

"It seems the drug dealers have set up major bases of operation in Aqua Anu. Just this morning a police station was bombed and despite increases in military force, we may be in this one for a while. Had it not been for the events last month making national news, we could have continued the smaller scale, quieter war, but the public wants change now."

"And with rumors of high ranking officials taking drug money, I cannot blame them." the President responded. "Speaking of rumors, has Coltarin or Haishan moved onto the recently collapsed nation of Istegium yet?"

"Nothing official, we have been observing Coltarin troops and naval movements since the first collapse but so far things have remained quiet." This time it was Primary Admiral Jesus Consuelo who responded, unlike Canarias, he spoke straightforward and with the same seriousness as the President.
"I have dispatched two Marine divisions to Booth Island, they will be passing Istegium on route and can be deployed to gain some ground if we need to take it in a hurry. I hope, however, we can reach a political end to secure a supply zone on the continent."

The room was taken aback slightly, out of all the things they expected, they had not believed the President would be as uninvolved as this.

If they went after former Istegium, they would have to contend with the all of us on this continent. It is something to keep an eye on, but I believe our best course of action is to declare neutrality, and focus our attention on issues closer to home. Mainly the North East, we have Belmotin to handle, drug wars to quell, and negotiate a supply zone on Istegium with parties who believe we have no purpose there. We will observe the Stevid situation for now."

"Aye Aye Mr. President"

Northern Eldrich, Stevid’s most northerly province, was bleak at the bet of times but today more so – especially on the north coastline. The weather really could not decide whether or not to just be grim or to rain as well. The surf from the sea mixed with the light drizzle and the black threatening sky added to the depressing backdrop. Along the soaked shale beach walked to men, rifles in hand, with full webbing and daysacks on their backs patrolling the coast. Not since the war with the Golden Throne had this practice been exercised but with the gearing for total war with Duke in New Empire and now an emerging threat from the North in the form of Imbrinium, the Territorial Army had been given the sad task of patrolling and keeping visual watch over the skies and sea. Regardless of modern radar and sensors, nothing compared to a decent set of eyeballs.

Across the grey sea a destroyer and two corvettes were making their way further north on an anti-submarine patrol. This was no longer a rare sight, neither were sighting of whales accidently making their way up rivers because the Navy was so busy pounding the water with acoustic 3D sonar blasts – enough to drive any animal close to insanity. This had paid off further afield though. A small ASW echelon off west Stevidian South Greal had chased away a possible enemy submarine but had not followed up with actual weapons fire. The seas around Stevid were probably the best defended in the region with the Navy so actively keeping watch.

The Territorial soldiers continued on their patrol towards one of the renovated concrete bunkers that served as a basic look out post only a mile away. Helping them plod on though was the faintest sound of bagpipes in the distance coming from the post – above it flew the Stevidian flag, violently being tossed around by the ocean wind. Grim indeed.

* * *


Three naval Sabre fast jets soared over the pristine ocean just underneath the clouds. They belonged to the Sanctus Mare class super carrier HMS Impervious operating from the battle fleet stationed south east of Morrdh, the same fleet that had been deployed as a counter measure to any Duke or Lyran shipping during the war with New Empire and also the Tehver Crisis. News had spread across the Stevidian military of a massive build up of Imbrinium naval assets. Surprise was the only thing that couple with this news, all this over a redeployment of military hardware to an under defended Imperial territory.

In response the Holy Empire decided to take a firmer but less aggressive approach to dealing with Imbrinium. These three Sabres were part of that plan of diplomatic action. The Defiler Class command and control cruiser operating from out of the battlefleet some seventy miles away had been busy trying to intercept enemy transmissions, and decode any that it did mange capture. Work was intensive and difficult, but luck, intelligence from other sources and Stevid satellite espionage (that was over a week away from having enough satellites in the right place to build and complete picture) had allowed several transmissions to be partially heard and cross referenced with images and other information from Army intelligence back in Morrdh. It had appeared that of all the task forces deployed by Imbrinium, several were heading in the direction of Stevid and South Greal but one in particular was within the immediate strike range of this task force. Further images had confirmed that the task force lacked a carrier, or at least it did not have one attached to it at this time. Regardless a collection of Imbrinium ships, ranging from corvette to cruiser, had been identified and was now being intercepted by these three carrier strike jets.

Intercept was achieved within 20 minutes but it was not lost on the pilots that a direct approach would be viewed as openly hostile. The jets computers registered radar waves washing over them, they had been detected and could possibly be within weapons range. The jets began an indirect approach flying in close formation as they circled the task force from afar. Slowly they closed the circle to a more intimate distance and then proceeded to fly towards the task force head on. The reason being that the jets presented themselves but remained less threatening because of the head on approach. To fly towards amidships would present a large target area to hit, where as from the bow head-on, the target area was narrow and less obvious… at least that was the idea anyway.

The jets, still in formation, accelerated to max power and closed the distance considerably. The computers panicked as weapons lock from the Imbrinium task force had been identified but the planes remained straight and true, committed to going as fast as possible. Assuming that the largest of the vessels in the fleet was the commanding vessel, the jets headed for that vessel – still accelerating. In an act typical of Stevidian sabre-rattling, though not seen since the Cold War with Kanami, the jets passed over the top of the cruiser by only two hundred metres and broke the sound barrier. Three huge sonic booms bombarded the fleet below as the jets turned and began circling – but the weapons lock remained and yet the planes continued to goad.

Slinking silently along the calm ocean about a kilometre away was a small box floating on the surface. Inside were optic cameras and sensors as well as a delicate communications array built on top. The technology was hardened and robust but small enough to remain undetected. Below the water line the object was bigger and housed more sophisticated batteries and optical lenses that could search the surrounding surface and underwater area with infrared beams and a small selection of other optics. Also under the waterline were small propellers for movement and fins for stabilisation. This box was had an optical firewire cable encased in steel that travelled deeper underwater, tethered to a Stevidian Astute Class submarine. The little box allowed the occupants within to view the scenario unfolding on the surface with high-definition quality, the ability to electronically identify the class of vessels and transmit this to the Defiler command ship. The ability to zoom

Stevid’s submarine force was probably the most secretive in the world; the government had only ever admitted that the Stevidian navy operated submarines. The buck stopped there. Classes, types, numbers, locations, service records, actions in times of war, none had ever been mentioned in parliament or in public. Even Stevidian allies got the vaguest information relating to Stevidian subs, usually that they were operating in theatre; this theatre could involve half an ocean. This most guarded secret meant it would always be difficult for foreign states to make grandeurs claims of a sinking or even that they had intelligence on their whereabouts. But any self respecting Admiral in the Imbrinium Royal Navy would know that Stevidian submarines were out there watching them. He would never know how many or where they were – but he knew they would be out there acting almost independently of Stevid itself, and that they would be a constant threat throughout this little cold war.


* * *


In response to the massive naval build up from Imbrinium, one Splinter Fleet and three independent task forces were activated to wartime readiness. A splinter fleet was basically half a battle fleet and could contain up to four task forces each with two carriers each. Each splinter fleet was led by an Inquisition Class dreadnought (significantly smaller than a super dreadnought and build to replace all the command battleship goliaths the Empire used to use) as flagship but not a central hub of command. The command structure was decentralised to protect the fleet’s combat efficiency so that task force operated under their own chain of command but answered to direct orders from the flagship when required.

The splinter fleets task forces were scattered around the Stevidian Isles bar one that was just north of Liberated America. The fleets would semi converge (as in be within thirty miles of one another) and head north to combat to combat the build up of Imbrinium naval units that were making their own journeys south.

Of the three independent task forces, one would make towards the north east of Independent Hitmen and patrol Stevidian shipping lanes north east of her ally. The two other task forces would make for the west coast f Stevidian South Greal to deter further enemy moves along that side of the continent.

With the move of the whole 5th Fleet to Stevidian South Greal, naval logistics were stretched relatively thin so a massive naval mobilisation coupled with a planned move of over 500 ships was straining naval planning and war mobilisation. Ideally another entire fleet would be ordered to full combat readiness and prepped to sail north in dribs and drabs. This could no longer be achieved. It was then publically announced that the 1st Fleet was to be placed on ‘Permanent Standby’ and that vessels under the bracket of the 1st Fleet were to begin training their crews for the possibility of war within a few weeks – commanders were immediately begin to liaise with their chain of command about potential moves and combat drills. It sounded serious but around 40% of the 1st Fleet, including the super dreadnought HMS Imperious and 60% of the carrier force, were in harbour at basic combat readiness and unable to do much more within the proper logistics at sea. The other 60% were already part of the on going patrols around the Stevidian Isles and local territories of Guffingford and Liberated America and were ready for war.

It was propaganda if anything and once the 5th Fleet’s move to South Greal was complete then the 1st Fleet would mobilise. Until then, enough was done in Stevid to make it look like mobilisation of the fleet to combat readiness through vehicle movements to harbours was on going. In truth little was being done.

Ord, Southern Morrdh

Southern Command, or to give it it's proper name of Commonwealth Forces Combined HQ South, was the Tri-Service command bunker located in the city of Ord on the Ordic Peninsula. Its area of operations included the southern approaches to the Straits of Mordent (a task shared with Commonwealth Forces Mordent) and the southern Morridane coastline stretching from the capital of Morrdun down to the Peninsula, then the Gulf of Morrdh coast to the Killian border where the Border Defence Force took over. All told it was almost three thousand miles of coastline and the surrounding waters that the Command had responsibility for, including the strategically important southern approaches to the Straits. With the diverting of merchant traffic to Morrdh's northern ports, there was now only naval ships and the odd fishing trawler that now sailed through these southern waters.

Traditionally Southern Command had concerned itself with the movements of naval vessels of the Commonwealth's neighbour of the Imperial Republic of Hailandkill, whilst warships were typically easy to spot Killian submarines were another matter entirely and so anti-submarine patrols was a major part of Southern Command's operations. With rising tensions between Immbrinium and Stevid, Southern Command had focused its attention to the Straits of Mordent to either submarines of either side from attempting to sneak through what were Morridane territorial waters in order to gain an advantage on the other side. Dozens of ships and patrol aircraft were devoted to the task, all overseen and co-ordinated by the joint Naval/Air Staff at Southern Command with the help of charts and intelligence reports.

With the Stevidian Royal Navy actively blasting the seas with sonar, it made things a little difficult for Morridane ASW efforts as each reading had to be double checked to see if it was a return of a Morridane sonar or an echo of a Stevidian sonar. These difficulties aside, Southern Command was aided greatly by the efforts of Naval Intelligence in at least attempting to keep track of naval activity in the vast ocean south-east of Morrdh. An intelligence report would come in of a possible unknown in the Southern Approaches, a brief check of the positions of Morridane naval units would be made before deciding whether or not to investigate. If it couldn't be confirmed then a patrol aircraft would be directed to the contact's last reported position and given a search grid to operate in, the aircraft would conduct its hunt with sonar buoys until pinpointed the contact. Then a mock attack would be made against the contact with 10lb practice bombs designed to harmlessly explode on hitting the water, usually the contact got the impression that the Morridanes meant business and altered course.
