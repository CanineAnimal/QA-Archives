My rating system goes something like:
10: Non-existent in music (so far), rare exceptions elsewhere that I look back and wonder if they truly deserve the 10 I gave them anyway.
9: great, but rare
8: Good. Likely to buy at some point but there are a lot of 8's so I have to prioritize (Hence different ratings in the 8 realm)
7: Good. Less likely to care to buy the album but in rare occasions will probably buy an album in this realm (Lorde and Birdy. Good casual listens when I need something similar)
6: Decent, but not likely to give it much more thought beyond this. Maybe with some rare exceptions with good songs in albums that get 6.
5: Pretty much my category for it not being bad technically, but I consider it to be boring or very boring. Like my opinion on Random Access Memories by Daft Punk. However, there were about 3 songs I would give 8 or 9's on this album out of 13, but I guess that wasn't enough to up it to a 6.
Anything below this I usually stop listening to before actually finishing the albums, as I don't generally care to torture myself with stuff that bad. Same with movies. I haven't watched many horrible movies because I just avoid anything that looks awful. However, a couple of random picks have been pretty bad.

That's a good system. Mine is similar:

5/5 - The best of the best. These are the pinnacles of the genre/time they fall under. Why aren’t you listening to them? Get them. Now.
4.5/5 - Very, very, very good. Amazing. Albums under this rating may have only a flaw or two preventing them from reaching the 5.0 status.
4/5 - Really remarkable. These are highly enjoyable, to say the least. I will return to these albums many times.
3.5/5 - Albums at this rating and above are albums I personally recommend. You can call this my "above average" rating. While the albums under this ranking are not life-changing, they are very solid and worthy of mention.
3/5 -These albums are acceptable but did not make more than a mildly positive impression on me. Enjoyable for one listen, but I probably won't return to it.
2.5/5 - Not so sure about how I feel about this. I’m drawn between the middle of bad and good for these albums.
2/5 - A poor work that isn't enjoyable, but I've heard worse.
1.5/5 - Pretty bad.
1/5: Just what were they thinking when they did this? They couldn’t possibly be serious. But there ARE worse, right?
0.5/5 - The absolute bottom of the barrel. Avoid like the plague.

I use two systems simultaneously, 0-5 for individual songs and 0-100 for whole albums.
5/5: I like this song very much, it might have flaws but good parts outweigh the bad parts.
4/5: Not bad but I'm not going to listen this one too many times. Not going to skip if it starts playing, especially when listening to full albums.
3/5: Pretty meh, very likely to get skipped. Listenable if being with friends and they like it
2/5: Bad song. Listenable? See 3/5.
1/5: Terrible, terrible song. Instant skip.
0/5: The song isn't even worth 1/5.
I rate all songs like this, to get my X/100 scores, I turn the scores like this: 5 = 100, 4 = 80, 3 = 50, 2 = 20, 1 = 10. I used to make 3 = 60, 2 = 40, 2 = 20 but it gave too high scores for shitty albums.

100: Excellent album I loved every single song here. Might have some flaws but good parts outweigh them greatly.
90: Good album, will listen a lot.
80: Has good songs but also few not so good songs.
70: Probably one or two excellent ones, quite many mediocre songs and one or two downright shitty ones.
60: Maybe one perfect song and the rest is more or less shit
50: Same as 60 but might lack that perfect one. Equals silence.
Less than 50: I'd rather stay in silence than listen to anything below this.
