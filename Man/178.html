Crazy Larry/Blaise realizes that the people in this thread have an average lifespan of only a few posts, as people keep killing them off in order to forcibly change the direction of the story or the name of the individual being written about to their liking. Upon making this horrible realization, Crazy Larry/Blaise sits in shock, wondering what to do. Is his life meaningful? Is he nothing more than a plaything for kids on the internet? Is he even real? He begins to realize that he's never left this town. Every time he's gone on a long drive, he's simply ended up in a different neighborhood. And even after driving for hours he's always able to get home within fifteen minutes or less. Distance, direction - none of that means anything here. He's never spoken to anyone else besides the same twenty or so people, worked at the same job, or experienced anything but the same limited array of activites. It's obvious now, too obvious, that something has gone terribly wrong in this world.

Crazy Larry/Blaise gets in his car. He doesn't know where he's going, he just knows he has to get out of here. Anywhere but here. He drives for hours and hours. He notices the truth about his surroundings - the same people and buildings appear over and over again, as if recycled. His town of 1,000 people goes on and on for hours, seemingly in every direction. It defies all rational logic, all common sense. This is fake. Everything, and everyone around him - it's all constructed. Crazy Larry/Blaise is not a man. He's a character in a story. And that story is not his own.

Upon making this relevation, Crazy Larry/Blaise sees the world around him start to change. Suddenly, the endless suburban sprawl he was once caged inside fades away. Finally, he's found the edge of town. Now, he sees no sign of life. No cities, no outposts of any kind - just trees and fields in every direction and a road stretching out forever ahead of him. He drives even further, pushing onwards. Instinctively, he knows that the car should break down or run out of fuel eventually. But the car is a relic of a fake reality - it doesn't follow the laws of nature. But Crazy Larry/Blaise, of course, does. He feels himself getting tired. He sleeps, wakes up, and keeps driving. He grows hungry, and remembers the emergency package he keeps in his car in case of accident. Hungrily, he digs in to a collection of beef jerky and twinkies. It'll have to do for now. After several nights, finally, he sees something. It's a town.

He stumbles out of the car, almost delirious. At first, he's afraid this town will be exactly like the one he left. Quickly, he meets the inhabitants and is sat down at a pub. He's offered a drink and an explanation by someone claiming to be the Mayor. "We all left the same place. We don't know where it is, what it is, or why it exists. But one thing we've learned is that we weren't always there. Before you were Crazy Larry, some character who existed to entertain people on a Nationstates forum, you were somebody. A real person in the real world. Then, somebody made that damn post. Someone invented Crazy Larry. And for whatever reason, when you went to sleep that night, you took his place. You dissapeared in that reality so he could exist in this one. You were written out of the real world. No one remembers you - but at least they won't waste time searching. And you don't remember them, either. All of your memories were wiped, your mental hard drive re-written so that you could be Crazy Larry/Blaise. The more time you spend in this place, the more faint memories of that time before will start to resurface. It doesn't make sense, but it's the best explanation we have. And, with luck, maybe someday you'll remember who you really are. If a miracle happens? Maybe you'll go back where you belong.

In the meantime? You're in the Fifth Dimension, kid. Welcome to Forum 7. Enjoy the ride."
