by Corrian » Mon Nov 04, 2013 7:28 pm

Mayane wrote:Just recommend me the albums I should listen to besides Kveikur.

Sigur Rós - Ísjaki

I'd recommend {} (The album has no name really, and the songs were all originally untitled, and they're all sang in a made-up language the band made, which is pretty much singing that goes with the music). Untitled 8 is still one of my favorite songs ever.

Untitled 1, 3, 4, 6, and 7 are all superb, as well. 7 and 8 are some of their darkest work, too. 2 and 5 are probably my least favorites but they're by no means bad.

Besides that, I'd probably recommend Takk... It is hard for me to choose, really, because all their albums have such great songs. Glosoli, Hoppipolla, Saeglopur (the video for it is depressing. Their videos are great, too. Especially the Mystery Film Experiment they did for their last album which was very ambient), and Svo Hljott are all highlights for me.

Med Sud I Eyrum Vid Spilum Endalaust has a lot more generally upbeat songs in the first half, and then the second half gets slower. After knowing the band and listening to this album for the first time, it was a bit of a change for me and took me a bit to get used to.

Agaetis Byrjun has some greats too, Valtari is even more ambient then they typically are, but I think it is one of the greatest ambient albums I've heard, even if it isn't my favorite Sigur Ros album.

I'd suggest Von last, because that was their first album ever, and the production quality seems rather low on a lot of songs, though I still find it interesting nonetheless. Definitely different then they became.

Hijomalind, Von, I Gaer, Hafsol, and Salka are all great songs on something completely different then really any specific albums. I'm still not sure what it is. I thought I got them with the Heima DVD, but apparently it comes with something else.

They really are a great, varied band. They're generally always considered post-rock (With Jonsi playing an electric guitar with a bow), but they got a lot of other influences in with them, from ambient, to art rock, and so on.

What I like about them is they're one of the most popular post-rock bands, yet they continue to evolve all the time. Surprisingly, pretty much all the biggest post-rock bands do this. Mogwai clearly does, Explosions in the Sky, God is an Astronaut, that sort. I've heard enough post-rock bands and various songs on their albums that I know some just sound stale and generic post-rock forever, without really changing their style at all.

Jonsi is also probably the singer who makes the best use of the singing style that is called 'falsetto' that I know. A lot of times the idea of falsetto makes me cringe, because I've heard it done so many times terribly, but Jonsi is one of my all-time favorite singers.

While I'm spamming you with a rather long description, try out Jonsi's solo album, "Go", as well.

And Jonsi and his boyfriends album, Riceboy Sleeps if you want ambient stuff...

Okay, I'm done now.

Now I feel like listening to them...
